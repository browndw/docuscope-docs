About the Elsevier Academic Articles Corpus

The Elsevier Corpus provides access to published academic research across a wide range of scientific disciplines. This corpus is built from Elsevier’s open-access articles, offering students the opportunity to analyze real published research writing from leading international journals.

What’s in the Elsevier Corpus?

The Elsevier corpus is drawn from a larger collection of over 40,000 open-access articles. For DocuScope analysis, this has been carefully curated to include 20 randomly sampled articles from each of 20 academic disciplines, creating a balanced representation of contemporary scientific writing.

The corpus covers major academic fields including:

  • Arts and Humanities - History and philosophy of science, science studies
  • Biochemistry, Genetics and Molecular Biology - Cell biology, genetics, biochemistry
  • Business, Management and Accounting - Business research, management studies, tourism
  • Chemical Engineering - Process engineering, materials science, catalysis
  • Chemistry - Analytical chemistry, electrochemistry, materials chemistry
  • Computer Science - Software engineering, pattern recognition, networks
  • Decision Sciences - Transportation research, health informatics, data analysis
  • Economics, Econometrics and Finance - Economic modeling, financial analysis, policy studies
  • Engineering - Energy systems, refrigeration, mechanical engineering
  • Environmental Science - Climate science, pollution control, sustainability
  • Materials Science - Nanomaterials, polymers, surface science
  • Mathematics - Applied mathematics, computational methods, statistics
  • Medicine - Clinical research, medical devices, health interventions
  • Neuroscience - Brain research, cognitive science, neurological studies
  • Physics and Astronomy - Applied physics, materials physics, energy research
  • Psychology - Cognitive psychology, behavioral research, clinical psychology
  • Social Sciences - Geography, political science, education, social policy

Why Use the Elsevier Corpus?

The Elsevier corpus offers unique advantages for studying academic writing:

  • Published Research Quality - All texts are peer-reviewed, published articles representing high academic standards
  • Contemporary Writing - Articles span 2014-2020, showing current academic writing conventions
  • Disciplinary Breadth - Balanced sampling across 20 fields enables cross-disciplinary comparisons
  • International Scope - Research from institutions worldwide, reflecting global academic discourse
  • Genre Consistency - All texts are research articles, allowing focus on disciplinary rather than genre differences

Research Questions You Can Explore

The Elsevier corpus is ideal for investigating:

  • Disciplinary Writing Conventions: How do STEM fields differ from humanities in their language use?
  • Cross-Field Analysis: What linguistic features distinguish business writing from chemistry writing?
  • Academic Register Studies: How does published research writing compare to student writing?
  • Citation and Hedging Patterns: Which fields use more cautious language (“may suggest”) vs. confident claims?
  • Technical Vocabulary: How does specialized terminology vary across disciplines?
  • Research Reporting: How do different fields present methodology, results, and conclusions?

  1. Choose Your Focus: Decide whether to compare broad disciplinary families or specific fields

  4. Think About Applications: Consider how academic writing conventions might inform your own research writing
